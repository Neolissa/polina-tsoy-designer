About me

Not just a designer. Product partner.

I - Pauline, Senior Product Designer with a focus on research and complex systems. Over 10+ years in design, I’ve come to a simple truth: the best interface is the one that solves the user’s problem and helps the business grow.

My interest is in the “kitchen” of the product: in the formation of hypotheses, searching for data for decisions and building processes, where design is not a stage, but part of the development cycle. How I work

My process is not a linear conveyor, but a loop

1

Understand the context

Immersion in the business model, metrics, users. We formulate a hypothesis, not technical specifications.

2

Explore & Prototype

Quick sketches, user interviews, testing paper prototypes. We are looking for insights before detailed design.

3

Design the system

Development of logic, flow and UI, which will form the basis of the design system. It's the decision that matters, not the "beauty."

4

Launch & Learn

Active development support, QA, analysis of first data after release. Ready to iterate.

Case Study WUW

Product evolution through a systematic approach to design

Context: Platform for personal growth. 5000+ users.
My role: Lead Product Designer (from research to development).
Problem: Chaos in the interface (200+ styles), slow development speed, impossibility of White Label.
Solution: Systematic approach: 35 reusable components, tokens, evolutionary refactoring.
Result: -90% styles, ×3 development speed, White Label open, consistency 95% → 95%.
Key decision: Instead of redrawing from scratch, I created a constructor from which 80% of the interface is assembled.

Case Study TVIP

Cross-platform media service design

Context: Media service with a ready-made backend, outdated interface. 5 platforms.
My role: Product Designer. From research to systems design.
Problem: A single interface for everyone: TV viewing, individual smartphone, background tablet, PC archive.
Solution: Profile system for 4 people, 2 color themes (day/night), UI-kit for 5 platforms, onboarding.
Result: Personalized profiles, automatic theme switching, consistent experience across all devices.

Case Study Relaunch

Case of an internal platform for employees

Context: Corporate platform. 100% of the company's employees.
My role: Product Designer (lead). From research to implementation.
Problem: Disparate tools (5+ systems), outdated interface, lack of social interaction and gamification.
Solution: Creation of a unified ecosystem with 25+ functions, design grooming, design system of 50+ components, social functions and gamification.
Result: ×4 session time, +40% engagement, 60% mobile activity, 100% adoption. 80% of UI problems were solved in real time.

Case Study DocsBird

Launching an international product under severe restrictions

Context: International product for document management. Europe and the Baltics.
My role: Product Designer (lead). From research to testing.
Problem: Launch a product from scratch in 1 year, taking into account regulatory requirements and cultural differences.
Solution: Creation of a separate brand and design system, adaptation for international markets. Reducing 7 registration steps to 3.
Result: Successful launch in 4 countries. Conversion +45%. Positive feedback about the trustworthiness of the interface.
Key decision: The transition from complex B2B flows to simple self-service solutions through a systematic approach to design.
