Challenge: launch a product that doesn't exist

Context and limitations

DocsBird is an international document management product that needed to be launched in the European and Baltic markets. The main difficulty: the product as such did not exist. It was necessary to create it from scratch, taking into account strict regulatory requirements, language characteristics and cultural differences.

Limited time (1 year) and resources forced us to take an unconventional path: instead of a complete redesign, we had to create a separate brand and design system that would work in parallel with the existing infrastructure.

“This was a task to create a product within a product: from the brand to the interface, where every pixel had to work on the user’s trust in the new market.”

Research: Understanding how markets really differ

Market analysis

Research of 5 key competitors in Europe and the Baltics. Identify differences in local patterns, user expectations and regulatory standards.

  • Audit of 30+ products
  • Study of GDPR and local laws
  • Analysis of pricing strategies

Key insight

The main difference is not in the interface, but in trust. Users in the Baltics and Europe are more conservative towards financial products. They need not just functionality, but confidence in security and transparency.

Conclusion: Design should work for trust, not shortcuts.

Visual testing

A/B testing 3 color schemes with focus groups in 4 countries. Determining the most neutral and trusting palette.

Green color is a symbol of reliability

Strategy: don’t copy, but rethink

Vertical timeline implementation

1

Audit - Based on the UI-kit structure

Instead of developing from scratch, I analyzed the existing components of the product. I identified universal elements that can be adapted to new markets, and identified “blind spots” - where a completely new approach is needed.

2

Reassembly - Replaced the visual language

I moved away from dark, complex interfaces to a light, simple palette with an emphasis on green. Reworked the visual hierarchy to focus on actions rather than visuals.

3

Scaling - New Figma Library

She created a scalable system of components that made it possible to easily add new languages ​​(localizations) and adapt to new regulatory requirements without reworking the entire interface.

4

Branding - Logo Coordination

Instead of a complete rebranding, I developed a system of extensions: the DocsBird logo with flags and language tags, maintaining the recognition of the main product, but adding international context.

Result: a product that works in a new market

Design system

A scalable system of components has been created that allows the product to be adapted to new markets in 2-3 weeks instead of 3-4 months.

Successful launch

The product was launched in 4 Baltic and European countries. Positive feedback from the first 1000 users about the clarity and trustworthiness of the interface.

Visual language

A neutral and trusting visual system has been developed that does not conflict with local cultural characteristics.

Conversion +45%

Scenarios adapted

User scenarios have been completely redesigned to take into account local workflows and cultural considerations.

Time has shortened

Registration process

Conclusions: limitation as opportunity

Main lesson

Limitations are not an obstacle, but a direction. Strict regulatory requirements and cultural differences forced us to look for non-standard solutions, which ultimately became a competitive advantage.

What worked

  • Create a product that does not copy, but adapts to the market
  • Prove that a design system can be a business asset
  • Launch an international product from scratch in 1 year

What was difficult

  • Balance between localization and globalization of the product
  • Coordination of visual language with international and local teams
  • Maintaining brand awareness while adapting to new markets

For the future

  • Expansion into new markets (Scandinavia, Central Europe)
  • AI integration to automatically adapt the interface to local standards
  • Development of an ecosystem of partner integrations
